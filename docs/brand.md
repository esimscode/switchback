## The name

A **switchback** is how you climb a slope too steep to walk straight up: the trail turns, and every turn gains altitude. It names the product's core belief — a career change is not a restart. You keep the mountain you've already climbed; you take a smarter line up.

- **Tagline (primary):** Same mountain. Higher ground.
- **Alternates to test:** Every turn gains altitude. · The honest way up. · Climb by turning.

## What the brand must feel like

From the product identity (PRD §5) and DESIGN.md, in order of priority:

1. **Calm** — a trusted advisor, not a hype machine. No urgency mechanics, no gamification aesthetics.
2. **Honest** — the product's moat is candor (honest fit grades, gaps named plainly). The brand should look like it tells the truth: high contrast, no gradients doing fake depth, generous whitespace.
3. **Capable** — a tool made by and for technical people. Reads native on GitHub.
4. **Quietly warm** — pastel accents against monochrome; the warmth is in restraint, not saturation.

Anti-references: resume-builder SaaS (blue gradients, stock optimism), hustle-culture career apps, outdoor-gear brands (we borrow the trail metaphor, not the REI look).

## Logo territory

The name is a gift for a mark — explore in this order:

1. **The ascending path.** A zigzag line that climbs left-to-right. At the right angles, three or four segments of a switchback trail read as an abstract **S**. Single stroke weight, sharp or softly-rounded turns — test both. Must survive at 16px (favicon) and as a monochrome stamp.
2. **The contour cut.** Topographic contour lines with one switchback path cutting through — richer, editorial, maybe the wordmark lockup or social banner rather than the app icon.
3. **The elevation tick.** A minimal chevron/staircase suggesting gained altitude — fallback if the S-path reads too much like a generic zigzag (test against Zig, Zapier's old marks, heartbeat lines).

Wordmark: lowercase **switchback** in Geist (the product face) — test Medium vs. the DESIGN.md fine-weight approach; tight tracking per DESIGN.md display rules.

## Voice

Direct, specific, zero hype — the create-linkedin-post skill's rules are the brand voice rules: concrete over adjectives, honest status labels, no "thrilled to announce." The strategist speaks like a good cornerman: on your side *and* honest with you.
